What could be the predisposition to this illness of the mind ?

A school project by F20DV Group 1 students

Alzheimer is a terrible disease that affect the mind, gnawing bit by bit the memories of people afflicted by it. This affliction is found in people from every corner of the world, concerning everyone. It is one of the main challenge of this century for the medical field, as to this day, there isn't any treatment that could completely cure it.

The data that we use today come from Kaggle - a website that allows publication of copyright-free datasets.

→ Click to see the DataBase ←

This data contains 74,283 entries from 20 countries around the world, 25 attributes, and is the combinaison of two Health Reports.

You may now wonder how we sorted and use this complex data. To know more, follow us on this journey.

As today will only be a brief introduction to this matter, we carefully selected a few attributes which seemed to carry the most relevance and impactfulness. We were left with 3 distinct cathegories, along with the one for the index.

On this note, let's begin !

Percentage of people with Alzheimer per age

Percentage of people with Alzheimer per country

As you may see above, the very first chart display the percentage of people affected by Alzheimer by age and gender - while the second is by country. Those three elements are our index. If you wish, you can sort all the following charts with those. Click on one of the two line of the first chart to pick a gender. Click again to deselect. To choose an age interval, brush the X-Axis of the first chart. You can then edit your selection. For the country, simply click on one.

As you may sadly notice, those percentage seems to be quite high - and the age, that seems to be one of it's main causes, it's not something that can be stoped

This following barchart is focused on two categoris that are bit further away from our topic of disease, as, this time, it does not follow the topic of alments of the body
Indeed - here, we will focuse on social and external factors.
You may click on the button to switch between the two.

Last but not least, we will see the comorbidity diseases.
comorbidity = a disease or medical condition that is simultaneously present with another or others in a patient
We have a selection of 6 of those, where you can switch between them through the menu on the right.


Through all this data, which can be depressing, we have decided to show you the positive side.
As you can see, the impact of comorbidities on Alzheimer's disease is not alarming.
The data speaks for itself !
So whether you have diabetes or not, it will not affect your risk of developing Alzheimer's disease.
